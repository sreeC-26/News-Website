#The Arab National Hospital (Baptist):



The Arab National Hospital (Baptist) is a local institution that provides health care to residents of different areas of the Gaza Strip “Al-Baptist” is located in one of the residential squares in the Al-Zaytoun neighborhood in the Gaza Strip, and close to the west is the Church of “Saint Philip the Evangelist.” They are separated by Umm Al-Laymoun Street, which connects Palestine Square to the north and Askola Square to the south. It is one of the oldest hospitals in Gaza City and was established at the end of the 19th century AD.

It was the first of many hospitals that turned into massacres on October 17, 2023, due to the ongoing war between Palestine and Israel. On October 17, 2023, Israel committed one of the most horrific massacres in the Gaza Strip, as its aircraft targeted the Arab National Hospital (Baptist), leading to the martyrdom of more than 500 Palestinians, the majority of whom were women and children who took the hospital as a safe refuge from Israeli raids.

The air strike resulted in the killing and wounding of hundreds of Palestinians, and the bodies of some martyrs were transformed into scattered body parts and others were charred. In addition, a huge fire broke out in parts of the hospital, destroying a large part of it. This was not the first raid on Al-Baptist Hospital during the Israeli aggression on Gaza, as occupation aircraft targeted it on October 15, 2023, which led to the injury of 4 of its medical personnel, and the destruction of some of its departments.

Although what Israel did was considered a war crime they weren’t not punished for what they did which led to more and more massacres and more and more war crimes . Another famous massacre was al-Shifa Hospital.


#Al-Shifa Hospital under attack (Baptist):



Al-Shifa Hospital, properly known as Dar al-Shifa Hospital, is the largest medical complex and central hospital in the Gaza Strip, located in the neighborhood of northern Rimal in Gaza City in the Gaza Governorate. Last week, the Israeli army bombed an ambulance outside the hospital, part of a convoy that was meant to carry patients from Gaza City to the Rafah border crossing, so they could be treated in Egypt.

Fifteen people were killed in the attack, according to the Palestinian Ministry of Health, which had coordinated the journey with the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) in Gaza. Human Rights Watch (HRW) said the attack, which killed people inside and around the ambulance, should be investigated as a possible war crime.

“We are minutes away from imminent death,” Muhammad Abu Salmiya, al-Shifa Hospital’s director, told Bessan news from inside the besieged facility on Saturday, where operations had to be suspended after it ran out of power and fuel.
